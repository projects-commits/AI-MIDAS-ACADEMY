# DESIGN SYSTEM

## Design thesis

AI MIDAS ACADEMY should look like a dark research lab crossed with a blackboard learning environment and a market intelligence terminal. The site should feel serious, modern, slightly disruptive, and unmistakably not generic edtech.

## Core visual system

- Background: `#121212`
- Primary: `#ff4500`
- Secondary: `#ffd700`
- Main text: white or near-white
- Muted text: gray

## Typography

- Brand, topbar, buttons: Arial Bold or Arial
- Body and UI: Arial
- Blackboard notes, formulas, diagram callouts: Segoe Print with cursive fallback
- Do not depend on external font files

## Interface personality

The interface should feel like:

- a code academy for serious learners
- a financial research desk with discipline
- a football intelligence workstation
- a digital blackboard for models, mechanisms, and annotation

## Visual rules

- Prefer high contrast
- Use orange and gold with intention, not everywhere at once
- Keep the background dark enough to support research-desk focus
- Preserve strong hierarchy between navigation, study area, references, and utility zones
- Avoid playful or soft consumer-app styling

## Component guidance

- Navigation should feel precise and operational
- Lesson surfaces should support deep reading
- Diagrams should feel instructional, not decorative
- Callouts should communicate meaning such as warning, model, note, or mechanism
- Code-like or formula-like blocks may be used where they help structured reasoning

## Layout guidance

- Static-first layouts are preferred
- Design for long-form reading without making the site visually flat
- Pages should support side references, summaries, or structured learning aids when useful
- Course introduction pages should keep the hero and then move into a single-column stack of compact cards
- Lesson reading pages should prioritize a wide central study column when side rails add noise or split attention
- Avoid persistent two-column scrolling on course introduction pages when it makes orientation harder
- Keep responsive behavior stable and readable on mobile

## Motion guidance

- Motion should support orientation, hierarchy, and reveal
- Avoid excessive animation
- Use movement to reinforce a system feel, not a marketing feel

## Things to avoid

- bright casino aesthetics
- sportsbook cloning
- glossy gambling-brand tropes
- empty futuristic effects with no instructional function
- random color drift away from the core palette
- typography that breaks the brand's serious tone
